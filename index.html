ℹ️ Important Information

⚠️ Foods to STRICTLY AVOID

  • Raw Papaya & Pineapple - Contains latex and bromelain, may cause contractions
  • Unpasteurized Dairy - Risk of Listeria infection
  • Raw/Undercooked Eggs - Salmonella risk
  • High-Mercury Fish - Shark, swordfish, king mackerel, tilefish
  • Raw Sprouts - Bacterial contamination risk
  • Excess Caffeine - Limit to 200mg/day (1 small cup coffee)
  • Alcohol - Absolutely none, causes fetal damage
  • Raw/Undercooked Meat - Risk of Toxoplasmosis
  • Ajinomoto/MSG - Avoid processed foods
  • Sesame Seeds (Til) - May induce heat, use sparingly

🚨 STOP Exercise & Call Doctor IF:

  • Vaginal bleeding or fluid leakage
  • Severe abdominal or pelvic pain
  • Persistent headache or vision changes
  • Chest pain or difficulty breathing
  • Dizziness or fainting
  • Severe nausea with inability to keep fluids down
  • Fever above 100.4°F (38°C)
  • Decreased fetal movement (after week 20)
  • Regular painful contractions before 37 weeks
  • Calf pain or swelling (possible clot)

💡 Managing First Trimester Symptoms

🤢 Morning Sickness: Eat small, frequent meals. Keep crackers by bed. Ginger tea, lemon water, jeera water help.
😴 Fatigue: Rest when needed. Iron-rich foods help. Short walks boost energy.
🫨 Bloating: Avoid gas-forming foods. Eat slowly. Ajwain water or saunf helps.
🍋 Food Aversions: Don't force foods. Find alternatives. This usually passes by week 14.

🏃‍♀️ Safe Exercises by Energy Level

Low Energy Days

Gentle breathing, ankle rotations, pelvic tilts in bed, short 5-min walks

Normal Energy Days

15-20 min walks, prenatal yoga basics, gentle stretching, Kegel exercises

High Energy Days

30 min brisk walking, swimming, prenatal aerobics, stationary cycling
